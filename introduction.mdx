## What Are Zmanim?

*Zmanim* (זמנים, literally "times") are the halachic time-points that govern the Jewish prayer schedule throughout the day. Because they are derived from sunrise, sunset, and the length of the solar day — not a fixed clock — they shift every day and differ significantly depending on your geographic location. Classic authorities such as the Vilna Gaon (GRA) and Rabbi Avraham Gombiner (Magen Avraham / MG"A) each define these intervals slightly differently, which is why you will often see two values shown for the same prayer window.

Key zmanim include **Alot HaShachar** (dawn), **Netz HaChama** (sunrise), **Sof Zman Shma** (the latest time to recite the Shema), **Chatzot** (halachic midday), **Mincha Gedola**, **Plag HaMincha**, **Shkiat HaChama** (sunset), and **Tzait HaKochavim** (nightfall). Zmanim Today calculates all of these — and more — for your precise coordinates, refreshed automatically each day.

## How It Works

- **Grant location access** — when you first open the app your browser asks for permission to read your GPS coordinates. Tap **Allow** to enable automatic location detection, or type a city name to set your location manually.
- **Times are calculated** — your latitude, longitude, and local timezone are sent to the Hebcal API, which returns every halachic time-point for the current date.
- **Your dashboard loads** — the home screen displays a live countdown to the next upcoming zman, a sortable list of today's prayer times, a mini Jerusalem compass, and quick-launch buttons for the siddurim.
- **Install as an app** — use your browser's "Add to Home Screen" (iOS) or "Install App" (Android / desktop) option to pin Zmanim Today to your device for instant one-tap access, just like a native app.

<Note>
  Zmanim Today uses your GPS data exclusively on-device to calculate prayer times. Your coordinates are passed directly to the Hebcal public API over HTTPS and are never stored, logged, or shared with any external server by the app itself.
</Note>

## Data Sources

### Hebcal API

The [Hebcal Zmanim API](https://www.hebcal.com/home/197/jewish-calendar-rest-api) is a free, open-source Jewish calendar service that computes halachic times from latitude, longitude, and timezone inputs. Zmanim Today calls the Hebcal `/zmanim` endpoint directly from your browser each time it needs to refresh prayer times. The API returns times for all major zmanim in both GRA and MG"A opinions, plus special values such as candle-lighting and Havdalah on the relevant days of the week.

### Sefaria API

The [Sefaria API](https://www.sefaria.org/developers) provides free, open access to the full corpus of Jewish texts. Zmanim Today uses Sefaria to fetch and display three complete siddurim — *Siddur Edot HaMizrach*, *Siddur Ashkenaz*, and *Weekday Siddur Chabad* — including their Hebrew text, section structure, and table-of-contents metadata. Text is fetched and cached locally inside the app for fast, offline-friendly reading.
