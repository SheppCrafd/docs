Zmanim Today is a browser-based Progressive Web App that puts everything you need for daily Jewish prayer — precise halachic times, a live direction-finding compass, and three complete digital siddurim — right in your pocket.

## What Are Zmanim?

_Zmanim_ (זמנים, literally "times") are the halachic time-points that govern the Jewish prayer schedule throughout the day. Because they are derived from sunrise, sunset, and the length of the solar day — not a fixed clock — they shift every day and differ significantly depending on your geographic location. Classic authorities such as the Vilna Gaon (GRA) and Rabbi Avraham Gombiner (Magen Avraham / MG"A) each define these intervals slightly differently, which is why you will often see two values shown for the same prayer window.

Key zmanim include **Alot HaShachar** (dawn), **Netz HaChama** (sunrise), **Sof Zman Shma** (the latest time to recite the Shema), **Chatzot** (halachic midday), **Mincha Gedola**, **Plag HaMincha**, **Shkiat HaChama** (sunset), and **Tzait HaKochavim** (nightfall). Zmanim Today calculates all of these — and more — for your precise coordinates, refreshed automatically each day.

## How It Works

- **Set your location** — on first open, the Home screen prompts you to tap **Use My Location** (which asks the browser for GPS permission) or to search for a city by name. Your coordinates and timezone are saved locally so the app remembers them next time.
- **Times are calculated** — your latitude, longitude, and local timezone are sent to the Hebcal API, which returns every halachic time-point for the current date. Responses are memoised in `sessionStorage` so subsequent views load instantly.
- **Your dashboard loads** — the home screen displays a live countdown to the next upcoming zman, a customisable list of today's prayer times, a mini Jerusalem compass, and print buttons for today's and tomorrow's zmanim.
- **Install as an app** — pin Zmanim Today to your device for one-tap access. See [Installation](/installation) for platform-specific steps.

<Note>
  Zmanim Today calculates prayer times entirely in the browser. Your coordinates are passed directly to the Hebcal, BigDataCloud, TimeAPI, and Open-Meteo public APIs over HTTPS as needed, and are never logged, stored, or shared by a Zmanim Today server — the app has none of its own.
</Note>

## Data Sources

### Hebcal API

The [Hebcal Zmanim API](https://www.hebcal.com/home/197/jewish-calendar-rest-api) is a free, open-source Jewish calendar service that computes halachic times from latitude, longitude, and timezone inputs. Zmanim Today calls the Hebcal `/zmanim` endpoint directly from your browser when it needs times for a new location or date, and caches responses in `sessionStorage` for the rest of the tab's lifetime. The Hebcal `/converter` endpoint is also called to render the Hebrew date and parsha. The API returns times for all major zmanim in both GRA and MG"A opinions, plus special values such as candle-lighting and Havdalah on the relevant days of the week.

### Sefaria API

The [Sefaria API](https://www.sefaria.org/developers) provides free, open access to the full corpus of Jewish texts. Zmanim Today uses Sefaria to fetch and display three complete siddurim — _Siddur Edot HaMizrach_, _Siddur Ashkenaz_, and _Weekday Siddur Chabad_ — including their Hebrew text, section structure, and table-of-contents metadata. Text is fetched and cached locally inside the app for fast, offline-friendly reading.
