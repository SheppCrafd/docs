Every time value shown in the app is derived from a single Hebcal request and then adjusted by a handful of deterministic client-side passes. This page documents each of the 16 halachic time-points the app tracks — what it means, how it's computed, and which Hebcal field it maps from.

---

## Source of truth

All base times come from a single call to the [Hebcal Zmanim REST API](https://www.hebcal.com/home/197/zmanim-halachic-times-api):

```txt
GET https://www.hebcal.com/zmanim
    ?cfg=json
    &latitude=<lat>
    &longitude=<lon>
    &date=<YYYY-MM-DD>
    &tzid=<IANA timezone>
```

Hebcal returns ISO-8601 timestamps in the requested `tzid`. The app then renames Hebcal's `camelCase` keys to its canonical `snake_case`, runs three post-processing passes, and caches the result under `sessionStorage['zmanim_cache_v3']` keyed by `lat.toFixed(3),lon.toFixed(3),date,tzid` for the tab lifetime.

### Halachic "hour" (sha'ah zmanit)

Many zmanim are expressed in **halachic hours** rather than clock hours. A halachic hour is 1⁄12 of the daylight portion of the day, so its length varies by season and latitude:

- **GRA (Vilna Gaon)** — daylight spans **sunrise → sunset**, so 1 halachic hour = `(sunset − sunrise) / 12`.
- **MGA (Magen Avraham)** — daylight spans **alot hashachar → tzeit hakochavim**, producing longer hours and earlier morning deadlines.

Hebcal computes both flavours; the app surfaces both wherever they differ.

## The 16 zmanim

Ordered as they appear in `lib/zmanimSchema.js`. "Hebcal field" is the key returned in `data.times`.

### Dawn & Morning

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Alot HaShachar — עלות השחר">
    **Dawn.** The first perceptible light before sunrise.

    - **Formula:** `sunrise − 72 minutes` (fixed-clock, not seasonal).
    - **Hebcal field:** `alotHashachar` (Hebcal's value is overwritten client-side).
    - **Client override:** `fixAlotHashachar()` recomputes `sunrise − 72 min` from the returned `sunrise` timestamp so the value is consistent regardless of the opinion Hebcal used.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Misheyakir — משיכיר">
    **Earliest time to don tallit and tefillin** — when there is enough light to recognise a casual acquaintance from four cubits away.

    - **Formula:** Sun at ~10.2° below the horizon (Hebcal's default).
    - **Hebcal field:** `misheyakir`.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Sunrise (HaNetz HaChamah) — הנץ החמה">
    **Sunrise.** The moment the sun's upper limb crosses the visible horizon.

    - **Formula:** Standard astronomical sunrise (sun's center at −0.833° accounting for atmospheric refraction and solar radius).
    - **Hebcal field:** `sunrise`.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Sof Zman Shema (GRA) — סוף זמן קריאת שמע">
    **Latest time to recite the morning Shema** per the Vilna Gaon.

    - **Formula:** `sunrise + 3 halachic hours (GRA)`.
    - **Hebcal field:** `sofZmanShma`.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Sof Zman Shema (MGA) — סוף זמן קריאת שמע">
    **Latest Shema** per the Magen Avraham — the more stringent (earlier) opinion.

    - **Formula:** `alot_hashachar + 3 halachic hours (MGA)`.
    - **Hebcal field:** `sofZmanShmaMGA`.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Sof Zman Tefillah (GRA) — סוף זמן תפילה">
    **Latest Shacharit (Shemoneh Esrei)** per the GRA.

    - **Formula:** `sunrise + 4 halachic hours (GRA)`.
    - **Hebcal field:** `sofZmanTfilla`.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Sof Zman Tefillah (MGA) — סוף זמן תפילה">
    **Latest Shacharit** per the Magen Avraham.

    - **Formula:** `alot_hashachar + 4 halachic hours (MGA)`.
    - **Hebcal field:** `sofZmanTfillaMGA`.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Midday & Afternoon

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Chatzot — חצות">
    **Halachic midday** — the exact midpoint between sunrise and sunset (equivalent to solar noon at that longitude).

    - **Formula:** `(sunrise + sunset) / 2`.
    - **Hebcal field:** `chatzot`.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Mincha Gedola — מנחה גדולה">
    **Earliest time for Mincha.**

    - **Formula:** `chatzot + 0.5 halachic hours (GRA)` (≈ 30 min after Chatzot at equinox).
    - **Hebcal field:** `minchaGedola`.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Mincha Ketana — מנחה קטנה">
    **Preferred time for Mincha** — the ideal window.

    - **Formula:** `sunset − 2.5 halachic hours (GRA)`.
    - **Hebcal field:** `minchaKetana`.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Plag HaMincha — פלג המנחה">
    **The halfway point of the Mincha Ketana → sunset interval.** Earliest time one may accept Shabbat (and, for those who follow this opinion, daven Maariv early).

    - **Formula:** `sunset − 1.25 halachic hours (GRA)`.
    - **Hebcal field:** `plagHaMincha`.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Evening & Night

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Candle Lighting — הדלקת נרות">
    **Shabbat / Yom Tov candle lighting time.**

    - **Formula:** `sunset − 18 minutes` (fixed-clock).
    - **Hebcal field:** `candleLighting` when Hebcal supplies it; otherwise derived client-side by `fixCandleLighting()` from `sunset − 18 min`.
    - **Day rule:** Set to `null` on any day except Friday (`applyDayRules`, `dow === 5`). Communities that light early — 40 minutes or 30 minutes before sunset — will need to adjust manually; the app uses the common 18-minute convention.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Sunset (Shkiyat HaChamah) — שקיעה">
    **Sunset.** The moment the sun's upper limb drops below the visible horizon.

    - **Formula:** Standard astronomical sunset (sun's center at −0.833°).
    - **Hebcal field:** `sunset`.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Tzeit HaKochavim — צאת הכוכבים">
    **Nightfall** — three medium stars visible. On Saturday this same value is displayed as **Havdalah**.

    - **Formula:** Sun at **8.5° below the horizon** (Hebcal's `tzeit85deg`).
    - **Hebcal field:** `tzeit85deg`.
    - **Day rule:** On Saturday (`dow === 6`), `applyDayRules` sets a virtual `havdalah` field equal to this value; the label on the UI switches to "Tzeit HaKochavim / Havdalah".
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Tzait (72 min) — צאת 72 דקות">
    **Nightfall per Rabbeinu Tam** — the stringent opinion used for ending Shabbat by many communities.

    - **Formula:** `sunset + 72 minutes` (fixed-clock).
    - **Hebcal field:** `tzeit72min`.
    - **UI note:** Hidden on Saturday to reduce clutter on the full Zmanim page (the Tzeit / Havdalah row already covers Motzei Shabbat).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Chatzot Laila — חצות לילה">
    **Halachic midnight** — the exact midpoint of the halachic night.

    - **Formula:** `(sunset + next-day sunrise) / 2`, equivalent to `chatzot + 12 halachic hours`.
    - **Hebcal field:** `chatzotNight`.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Post-processing pipeline

Every Hebcal response — fresh or served from the session cache — is passed through three deterministic passes before the UI sees it (`hooks/useZmanim.js`):

```js
function postProcess(result, date) {
  return applyDayRules(
    fixAlotHashachar(
      fixCandleLighting(result)
    ),
    date,
  );
}
```

### 1. `fixAlotHashachar`

Overwrites Hebcal's `alotHashachar` with a computed `sunrise − 72 min` so the value is consistent across dates and locations regardless of which underlying opinion Hebcal used.

### 2. `fixCandleLighting`

If Hebcal already returned a `candleLighting` value (Fridays and Yom Tov eves), it is kept as-is. Otherwise, the app synthesises `sunset − 18 min` so the field is always populated on Fridays even when Hebcal omits it.

### 3. `applyDayRules`

Applied per render based on the selected date's day of week:

| Day of week | Rule |
| --- | --- |
| Friday (`dow === 5`) | `candle_lighting` kept; all other days it's set to `null` |
| Saturday (`dow === 6`) | `havdalah = tzait_hakochavim` (8.5°); all other days it's `null` |

This is why Candle Lighting and Havdalah rows only appear on their respective days — the fields themselves are `null` outside those days.

## Caching

Results are cached in `sessionStorage['zmanim_cache_v3']` keyed as:

```txt
<lat.toFixed(3)>,<lon.toFixed(3)>,<YYYY-MM-DD>,<tzid>
```

Coordinates are rounded to 3 decimal places (~110 m) so tiny GPS jitter doesn't invalidate the cache. Day-of-week rules are applied on read, so the same cached response can render correctly for any date — only the rendered `candle_lighting` and `havdalah` fields flip.

---

## Accuracy notes

- Times are computed by Hebcal using well-known solar algorithms and rounded to the second in Hebcal's response, then formatted to the minute in the UI. Expect ±1 minute of variance versus other reputable calculators.
- **Alot HaShachar** uses the fixed 72-minute definition. Communities using degree-based dawn (e.g. 16.1°, 19.8°) will see different values elsewhere; the app does not surface those.
- **Candle Lighting** uses the widespread 18-minute convention. Jerusalem (40 min), Haifa (30 min), Petach Tikva (~40 min), and other communities with local customs will need to subtract additional minutes manually.
- Coordinates are truncated to 3 decimals for caching but sent to Hebcal at full precision — the truncation only affects cache hit/miss, not the calculation.
